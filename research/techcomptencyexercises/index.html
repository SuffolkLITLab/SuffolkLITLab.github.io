Intro:

Interested in integrating technology competency skills into a legal practice course? Curious about why this is a valuable professional skill and ethical requirement for today’s modern lawyer in the context of fundamental basics of legal analysis, writing, and research? Associate Professor Dyane O’Leary, Director of Suffolk’s Legal Innovation & Technology Concentration answers these questions and provides pages of concrete suggestions and classroom ideas in five broad technology competency categories in her article available on SSRN: “Smart” Lawyering: Integrating Technology Competence Into the Legal Practice Curriculum. Contact Professor O’Leary at dloleary2@suffolk.edu if you’d like to discuss the exercises or get help shaping them to a particular course project.
