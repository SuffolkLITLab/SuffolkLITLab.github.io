Need Some Hackathon Help?

Associate Professor Dyane O’Leary, Co-Director of Suffolk’s Legal Innovation & Technology Concentration, explores intellectual property rights in the context of legal hackathons in her article available at SSRN—License to Hack. She channels the hackathon spirit of creating something useful with Suffolk’s LIT mission of focusing on helpful solutions for others to set up a repository for sample hackathon agreements. Whether you are hosting a hackathon next week or in the early planning stages for one, feel free to consult the publicly available sources here or adapt the Hackathon Participant Agreement template for your own use. This is not intended as formal legal advice, but rather a starting point for hackathon rookies or a refresher for those inspired to take a fresh look at their hackathon structure.

Sample Hackathon Participant Agreements

The following files were obtained through publicly available sources and are not limited to legal hackathons. The agreements are generally grouped into three categories: (a) IP remains with participants; (b) IP remains with organizer; and (c) middle ground license or option. Intellectual property provisions on each PDF file are highlighted in yellow.
