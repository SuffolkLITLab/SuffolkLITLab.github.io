Consequently, the broad-based concern expressed over the rise of algorithmic bullshit is both understandable and warranted. Large language modules which complete text by predicting subsequent words based on statistical patterns present in their training data are the embodiment of such bullshitters. They are by design fixated on producing plausible sounding text, and since they lack understanding of their output, they cannot help but be unconcerned with accuracy. Couple this with the fact that their training texts encode the biases within, and at worst one finds themselves with what some have called mansplaining as a service, overconfident assurtions colored with misoginy, racisum, and an uter lack of concern for facts.

So why did the LIT Lab build this tool, and why bother even working with such a bullshitter? For one, algorithmic BS artists lack agency. They do not understand their output, and they need not act in isolation. They arrange text based on conrtaints implied by their training data. Consequently, one can work to mitigate the problem of BS by providing context, e.g., by asking them to rearrange existing texts. Such is the trick used here. We have asked an LLM (GPT-3) to reorder the contents of the course cataloge in response to your questions. This of course does not limit the danger posed by such tools driving the cost of BS production to nearly zero. It does, however, hint at the possibility of pro-social uses.

The problem of BS, however, is not the only issue faced by such models. When they allow end-users to provide their own text, they are subject to prompt injection, the careful crafting of inputs to override a tool's original instuctions. We invite you to play with such incantations below.
